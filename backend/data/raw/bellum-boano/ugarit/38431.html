Caesar eodem itinere uti noluit ne nauibus in flumine dimicaret , sed circumuectus eo mari quod Africae partis esse dicitur , sicuti supra demonstrauimus , prius tamen regis copiis occurrit quam is Mithridaten aggredi posset eumque ad se uictorem incolumi exercitu recepit .
Caesar was unwilling to use the same route , so as to avoid fighting a naval battle in the river , but taking a roundabout route by the sea that is said to belong to a part of Africa , as I explained above , he nonetheless fell in with the king forces before the king could attack Mithridates , and welcomed a victorious Mithridates with his army intact .
